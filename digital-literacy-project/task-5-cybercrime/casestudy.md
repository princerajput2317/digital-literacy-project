# Case Study: UPI and Online Payment Fraud

**The Crime:** UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud involves cybercriminals tricking victims into authorizing financial transactions or revealing sensitive PINs, resulting in unauthorized direct debits from the victim's bank account.

**How it Happens:** 1. **The Hook:** The scammer contacts the victim, often posing as a legitimate entity (e.g., a customer service agent, a buyer on an online marketplace, or a college official demanding an immediate fee). 
2. **The Trick:** Instead of sending money, the scammer generates a "Collect Request" or sends a QR code to the victim. They instruct the victim to enter their UPI PIN, falsely claiming that doing so will *receive* the funds into their account.
3. **The Theft:** Because a UPI PIN is only ever used to *send* money or check a balance, the moment the victim enters their PIN, funds are instantly transferred from their account to the scammer.

**Who is Targeted:** College students are highly targeted due to their frequent use of UPI for daily expenses, peer-to-peer transfers, and online shopping. Scammers exploit urgency, such as threatening to cancel an enrollment or offering a heavy discount on textbooks.

**The Consequences:** Victims suffer immediate financial loss, which is often difficult to recover quickly. It also causes severe emotional distress, anxiety, and a loss of trust in digital financial systems.
